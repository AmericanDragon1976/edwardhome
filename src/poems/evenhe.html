    EVEN HE

    Even He,
    Who defeated this enemy,
    First fell beneath scythe's blow,
    But rose again and laid the reaper low.

    Even the King
    For whom Angels sing,
    First was swaddled in the hay,
    But still is Light and Life to the stray.

    Even the innocent
    Who was no longer infant,
    First felt Blindfold's sword and scale,
    But carried the weight, and bore it well

    Even mighty God
    Who is the Word,
    First, nailed up before eternity,
    But draws and judges all, justly!

    Even the Greatest
    Who heeded no hiss,
    First suffered the bite of dread,
    But finished, and crushed the serpents head.


    By Edward Moroni Goodson
    Summer 2010 Last revision (11 Jan 2011)


        The meaning in this peom is important to me, enough so that I will point it out even though I think that is is obvious for the most part. The reaper is death and it is death's scythe. Blindfold's sword and scale is lady justice refering to taking the weight of justice even though he himself was innocent. The hiss is the serpent's temptations and the serpent is the devil, who in the end will have know power because Christ finished his work and partook of the bitter cup.
